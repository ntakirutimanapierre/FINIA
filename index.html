Digital Financial Inclusion Index for Africa

Purpose of the Index

This index was developed to:

  • Simplify complex data: Condense multifaceted digital finance data into a single, comprehensible metric.
  • Track progress: Monitor changes in digital financial inclusion over time.
  • Enable comparisons: Identify leaders and laggards in digital finance adoption.

Components of the Index

  • Infrastructure: Based on the GSMA Mobile Connectivity Index.
  • Investment: Fintech investment data sourced from Crunchbase, normalized by GDP.
  • Literacy: Adult literacy rates from the World Bank.

Methodology

  1. Data Collection: Gathered from reliable sources like GSMA, Crunchbase, and World Bank.
  2. Feature Selection: Chosen based on relevance to digital financial inclusion.
  3. Weighting: Equal weights for each component; investment normalized by GDP.
  4. Validation: Factor analysis, internal consistency checks, and expert review.

Key Findings

2023 Index scores reveal interesting patterns:

  • Top Performers: Seychelles (57.14%), Mauritius (53.87%), South Africa (53.32%)
  • Lower Performers: Mali (21.76%), Burkina Faso (21.92%)

Island nations and smaller populations adopt fintech faster. Countries with poor connectivity and tight regulations fall behind.

Recommendations

  • Policy: Create supportive regulatory environments for fintech innovation.
  • Infrastructure: Invest in internet and mobile coverage to scale access.
  • Monitoring: Update the index regularly to guide data-driven policy.
